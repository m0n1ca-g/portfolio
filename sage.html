Sage UX Design Team

Determining the Demand for a Mobile Cooking App

We found that a significant & increasing amount of 25-34 year olds are using the internet - and, more specifically, mobile applications to discover cooking-related resources.

Understanding Our Users & Changing Direction

When we began, we intended to create an app where users could find recipes they could cook using ingredients they already had in their kitchen. After analyzing the data from our preliminary survey and qualitative interviews, we began to discover some interesting trends in the responses. People love to cook and do so very often, but they were also reporting significant pain points and stressors around cooking. We began to empathize with our users and became determined to design an app that could help them enjoy a more peaceful cooking experience. In order to propel us in this new direction with more applicative data, we conducted a secondary survey.

Identifying Pain Points & Cooking Behaviors with Quantitative & Qualitative Research

We learned so much about our users from our surveys and interviews...from how cooking fits into their social life and their favorite things to cook to their motivations and frustrations. We all loved hearing about the more positive emotions tied to cooking - the memories of cooking with loved ones or using a treasured family recipe. We did an affinity diagram and empathy map to bring focus and clarity, the results of which strengthened our motivation to change direction. Our research revealed a number of pain points and stressors - with the two most common themes being TIME and SKILL, or lack thereof!

With a new goal in mind we expanded our research to include not only recipe and cooking apps, but ones that encourage and promote mindfulness. We did SWOT analyses, identifying the Strenghts, Weaknesses, Opportunities & Threats to consider. We found plenty of cooking and recipe apps and several apps that encourage de-stressing, calm and meditation, but, other than article on the Headspace app, we couldn't find any apps that specifically focused on helping make cooking less stressful.

Sage makes cooking more meaningful. With one app home cooks can unwind with meditations and playlists designed specifically to help them get the most out of the time they spend in the kitchen. Learn about ways to pick foods that help reduce stress and find hints to make cooking easier and more enjoyable.
